A step to victory

The beauty of life is that we decide how we want each chapter of our life to look like. A step at a time can change the overall perspective of how one view life, it can alter ones mindset and change their entire lifestyle. I go on a hike whenever I need a change in altitude. The goal is never to get to the summit at a specific time but to focus on each step I make, each deep breath I take, the soothing and satisfying exhales. The sun kissing my skin, exotic plants that scream 'pick me, take me home with you', the waterfalls that feel like they wash away all burdens of the world, not forgetting rocky trails that mirror life. Hiking helps me to be in tune with myself and to connect with nature. After all, nature teaches and heals.

  • Lion's Head

    Lion's Head is a popular trail in Cape Town known for its stunning panoramic views of the city, Table Mountain, and the Atlantic Ocean. The trail offers a moderate hike with some rocky sections, but the rewarding summit makes it all worthwhile. It's a great spot to catch the sunrise or sunset and is loved by locals and tourists alike.

  • Newlands Forest

    Located on the eastern slopes of Table Mountain, Newlands Forest offers a tranquil escape from the bustling city. The trail winds through a lush forest with a variety of plant species and is perfect for nature lovers. It provides a range of hiking options, from easy strolls to longer hikes, making it suitable for all fitness levels.

  • Constantia Nek & Four Reservoirs

    This trail takes you through the picturesque Constantia Valley, known for its vineyards and natural beauty. Starting at Constantia Nek, you'll hike past four reservoirs, each offering its own scenic views. The trail is surrounded by indigenous vegetation and offers a peaceful and rejuvenating experience.

  • Pipe Track

    The Pipe Track is a classic coastal trail that hugs the slopes of Table Mountain and overlooks the Atlantic Ocean. It follows the path of an old water pipe and provides stunning views of the coastline and Twelve Apostles mountain range. The trail is relatively flat, making it a popular choice for walkers, joggers, and trail runners.

  • Devil's Peak

    Rising above Cape Town, Devil's Peak offers a challenging hike with rewarding views. The trail takes you through fynbos-covered slopes, rocky terrain, and a steep ascent towards the peak. Once at the top, you'll be treated to breathtaking vistas of the city, Table Mountain, and the surrounding landscapes.

  • Kasteelspoort

    Kasteelspoort is a scenic trail that starts from the lower cableway station on Table Mountain. The trail winds its way up the slopes, passing beautiful rock formations, indigenous vegetation, and a waterfall. As you ascend, the views become increasingly spectacular, providing a memorable hiking experience.

  • Cecilia Forest

    Nestled in the southern suburbs of Cape Town, Cecilia Forest offers a peaceful and shaded hiking experience. The trail meanders through dense forest, crossing streams and passing charming picnic spots. It's a great escape for nature enthusiasts looking for a serene hike close to the city.

  • Platteklip Gorge

    Platteklip Gorge is one of the most popular routes to the summit of Table Mountain. It's a challenging ascent with steep sections and a well-defined path. The gorge offers breathtaking views of Cape Town and its coastline, making it a favorite among hikers seeking a rewarding workout.

  • Signal Hill

    Signal Hill, situated next to Lion's Head, offers a relatively easy hike with stunning views of the city, Table Mountain, and the Atlantic seaboard. It's a great spot to watch the sunset or enjoy a picnic while taking in the panoramic vistas. The trail provides a refreshing escape from urban life.

    • These trails in Cape Town offer diverse landscapes and captivating views, ensuring unforgettable hiking experiences for those who explore them.
